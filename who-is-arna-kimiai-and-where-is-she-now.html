The Event That brought her To Public Attention

On the 7th of March 2021, around 12:45 p.m., an SF Bay Area Uber driver named Subhakar Khadka (Khadka is an immigrant from Nepal) picked up three female passengers. This happened in San Francisco, precisely at San Bruno Avenue.

The three ladies came dressed in varying colors of black, red, and yellow, and the lady in black wasn’t wearing a mask; her name is Arna Kimiai. The red-clothed lady’s name was later revealed as Malaysia King, and the other person in yellow remained unidentified to date.

Considering the safety measures from the then ravaging Corona Virus pandemic, Khadka respectfully asked Arna to wear a mask. After she refused, he canceled their ride as Arna’s behavior went against Uber’s rider policy that says every passenger must board their vehicle wearing a mask.

They were already partway through the ride when the altercation occurred. The driver’s decision provoked violent reactions from Arna Kimiai and her co-passengers. Still, Arna took it to the extreme when she coughed on Khadka, attempting to tear off his mask, and grabbed the driver’s mobile phone, which was lying on the center dash area.

Arna was still confronting Khadka when her friend, Malaysia King, pepper-sprayed him into the back seat of his cab. Even when the helpless driver managed to shoo the crazy girls out of his cab, Malaysia gave him one last pepper spray through the partially open car window.

Besides, they all seemed less concerned by the fact that there was a dash-mounted camera recording the whole incident in the car. This incident earned Arna the unpalatable moniker – “The Uber Bully Girl.”

Was The Uber Bully Girl Arrested?

The Uber driver, Khadka, wasted no time in sharing the video of his confrontation with Arna Kimiai and her clique, and within just one week, it went viral on the internet. The viral video led the Los Angeles cops to arrest Arna.

Reports said she was the one who surrendered herself to the authorities. “The Uber Bully Girl” presented herself at the San Francisco Women’s Jail around 7:00 p.m. on the 14th of March 2021, where she received four–counts charges.
