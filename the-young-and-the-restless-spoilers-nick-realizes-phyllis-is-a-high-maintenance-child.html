Nick Realizes Phyllis Is a High-Maintenance Child

Publish date: 2024-05-13

The Young and the Restless (Y&R) spoilers reveal that Nick Newman (Joshua Morrow) realized that Phyllis Summers (Michelle Stafford) is a high-maintenance child.

He took care of her when she had too much to drink. He brought her back to her hotel room. She asked him to stay, but he refused. Keep on reading to learn more.

Y&R Spoilers: Nick Cares For Drunken Phyllis

According to The Young and The Restless spoilers, Nick had to take care of Phyllis, who drank too much after seeing Danny Romalotti (Michael Damian) with Christine Blair (Lauralee Bell). He confirmed that they’re in a serious relationship.

Phyllis ended up drinking too much at the bar while with Nick. He offered to bring her back to her hotel room.

Nick had room service bring Phyllis a greasy hamburger. She tried to kiss him and get him to go to bed with her, but he turned her down. Nick realizes she acts like a high-maintenance child when she doesn’t get her way. This was brought up on the SoapCentral.com Message Boards.

The user Y&R25years posted: “Look at the high maintenance that comes with a child with Phyllis. Wow, these guys Danny and Nick just have a lifetime of dealing with Phyllis as she bounces from one baby daddy to another with her pity partying drinking binge.”

Y&R fans took to the thread to share their thoughts. Most were relieved that Nick turned Phyllis down. Others commended Michelle Stafford for pulling off the complex character of Phyllis Summers.

“Michelle Stafford is probably the most convincing actor on the show these days. She pulls off the complexities of Phyllis with her facial expressions, body language, and dialogue delivery better than any actor in the genre IMO. Any other actor would be ffwd [sic] material if given this character,” one user noted.

The Young And The Restless Spoilers: Phyllis Left On Her Own

According to The Young and the Restless spoilers, Phyllis was left on her own once again. He thought he could make her happy with greasy cheeseburgers, macaroni and cheese, and onion rings. But she wanted to keep her bed warm. Y&R fans agreed that it was a great scene with excellent acting.

“Nick did the right thing. Great scenes. Phyllis is drunk, depressed, and lonely. It would be a very bad idea to sleep with her in this mental state,” one fan wrote.

“How much longer do we have to see that piece of misery wallowing in her self-pity? Her gasping, snot running, sobbing, trying to seduce men performances are really stale… She’s long past any sympathy state with her self-pity,” another fan argued.
